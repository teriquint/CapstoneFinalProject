DCDA 40833 Capstone Project | Spring 2026

Welcome

Welcome to my capstone project website for DCDA 40833. This project examines how higher education programs market themselves across official websites, Instagram, and LinkedIn.

I am interested in how schools present different identities depending on the platform and how those patterns may vary by school type, geography, and institutional context.

Project Overview

This capstone project focuses on how colleges and universities communicate their value and identity across different digital platforms. Official websites, Instagram, and LinkedIn each have different audiences and different expectations, so schools may emphasize different themes depending on where they are posting.

The goal of this project is to compare those patterns and better understand how higher education institutions shape their digital identity online.

Research Question

Main question: How do higher education programs market themselves across official websites, Instagram, and LinkedIn, and how do those patterns vary by platform, school type, and geography?

Related focus: I am also interested in whether some platforms emphasize certain themes more than others, such as career outcomes, prestige, belonging, innovation, or return on investment.

Method

This project uses comparative content analysis. I am collecting examples from official websites, Instagram, and LinkedIn and organizing them in a spreadsheet for comparison.

Each example will be coded by platform, school, school type, geography, theme, and tone. This will allow me to identify patterns across platforms and across institutions.

Current Progress

So far, I have narrowed the project topic, identified the three platforms I want to compare, and started developing theme categories for coding. I am also working on the spreadsheet structure for organizing the data.

I am tracking themes that include career outcomes, prestige and rankings, student life, belonging and community, innovation, networking, academic quality, and return on investment. One obstacle that I have run into is the fact that social media accounts are not always set up the same way. Some universities have separate business school accounts that include both undergraduate and graduate content, while others use graduate-specific accounts for MBA or graduate programs. I am working through how to compare these accounts as consistently as possible so that I am making fair comparisons across institutions.
